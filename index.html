Where the threatened wild things are

These global biodiversity maps – the most detailed yet – reveal that hotspots of diversity and threat don't always go hand in hand: mammals are most diverse in the Amazon basin, yet the biggest threats to their survival are in Borneo, Sumatra and the Malay Peninsula – where habitat is being destroyed at an alarming rate.

Use the controls at top right to explore what remains to be saved from what some call the Earth's sixth mass extinction. Humanity takes the blame, eliminating species at about 1000 times the natural rate.

Source: BiodiversityMapping.org/IUCN/BirdLife International/NatureServe
